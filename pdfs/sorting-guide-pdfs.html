Sorting Guide

1. Clear Your Space

Before you begin sorting, remove anything that doesn’t belong in the area. This gives you a clean starting point and reduces overwhelm.

  • Throw away obvious trash
  • Remove dishes, laundry, or items from other rooms
  • Wipe the surface if needed

2. Sort Into Categories

Group similar items together so you can see what you have.

  • Keep
  • Donate
  • Trash
  • Recycle
  • Relocate
  • Unsure

Tip: Use bins, baskets, or even paper plates to keep categories separated.

3. Decide What Stays

Once everything is grouped, make decisions one category at a time.

  • Do I use this?
  • Do I love this?
  • Do I have duplicates?
  • Does this fit my current lifestyle?

4. Store With Purpose

Everything you keep should have a home.

  • Keep daily-use items easy to reach
  • Store rarely used items higher or lower
  • Use clear bins when possible
  • Label everything

5. Maintain the System

A sorting system only works if you revisit it.

Weekly Reset

  • Put items back in their homes
  • Empty your “unsure” bin
  • Recycle or donate items you no longer need

Monthly Check-In

  • Review categories that grow quickly
  • Remove clutter before it builds up

Bonus: Quick Sorting Checklist

  • ☐ Clear the surface
  • ☐ Gather sorting bins
  • ☐ Sort into categories
  • ☐ Make decisions
  • ☐ Put items away
  • ☐ Label storage
  • ☐ Do a quick reset

Bonus: What To Do With “Unsure” Items

If you’re stuck on something:

  • Put it in a 30‑day box
  • Write today’s date
  • If you don’t open the box in 30 days → donate it
