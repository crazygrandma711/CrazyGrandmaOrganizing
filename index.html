💡 Card-Making Tips & Tricks

Quick, helpful ideas to make your card-making easier, faster, and more fun.

⚠️ Common Card-Making Mistakes to Avoid

Little things that can make a big difference in how polished your cards look.

❌
Rushing the Adhesive
Not letting glue dry can cause shifting or warping.
❌
Forgetting to Test Ink
Always stamp on scrap first to check color and pressure.
❌
Overcrowding the Design
White space is your friend — let elements breathe.
❌
Not Matching Envelopes
Make sure your card fits the envelope before decorating.
❌
Skipping the Sentiment Test
Lay out your sentiment before gluing to avoid crooked placement.
❌
Using Too Many Patterns
Stick to 1–2 patterns so your focal point stands out.
✂️
Pre-Cut Your Bases
Keep a stash of A2 card bases ready to go.
🎨
Use a Color Palette
Pick 3–4 colors before you start to stay coordinated.
🗂️
Make a “Use First” Bin
Keep scraps, leftover die-cuts, and extras in one spot.
⭐
Add Dimension
Foam dots make any card look instantly polished.
🧼
Clean Stamps Right Away
Keeps them crisp and long-lasting.
📏
Use a T-Ruler
Perfectly straight sentiments every time.
📏 Types of Cards & Standard Sizes

A handy guide to the most popular card styles and the sizes crafters use most.

A2 Card
4.25" × 5.5"
Most common card size; fits standard envelopes.
A6 Card
4.5" × 6.25"
Great for photo cards and layered designs.
A7 Card
5" × 7"
Perfect for elegant, formal, or detailed cards.
Slimline Card
3.5" × 8.5"
Tall and stylish; great for scene cards.
Mini Slimline
3" × 6"
Cute, modern, and perfect for quick cards.
Square Card
5" × 5" or 6" × 6"
Great for bold layouts; requires extra postage.
Gatefold Card
5.5" × 8.5" (folded)
Opens from both sides; perfect for fancy designs.
Z-Fold Card
4.25" × 11" (scored twice)
Fun interactive style with layered panels.

A simple, helpful checklist to keep all your card-making supplies neat, tidy, and easy to find.

Detail scissors & regular craft scissors.

Tape runner, liquid glue, foam dots, glue dots.
